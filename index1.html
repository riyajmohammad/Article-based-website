Overview:

The "One Nation, One Election" initiative aims to synchronize all elections in India, ensuring they are held simultaneously.

The One Nation One Election word was firstly used by the Election Commission India in 1982 And Law Commision of India in 1999 and Indian Parliament Commission also talked about in One Nation One Election in 2015 and Narendra Modi also said that in One Nation One Election in India. One Nation One Election is a voting process in which all the Elections in the Country are Held at one go so that the Economy of the Country can be improved and those who stop Due to Elections can not stop development. Challenges:

Implementing this initiative faces challenges such as legal hurdles, opposition from various political parties, and logistical issues.

There are so many challenges faces as follow:
1: First challenge are to change the Constitutional Article 83 and Article 85 and total changes in Constitutional Article 18 changes.
2: To get it done, support of two-thirds of the MPs in the Lok Sabha is necessary, which is not easy.
3: Due to simultaneous Elections, smaller parties will not get time to campaign.
4: Holding elections simultaneously will increase expenses
